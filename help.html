üîç Understanding Search Results

üìä Similarity Scores (Semantic Search)

Each memory card shows a match score indicating relevance:

  • 90-100%: Highly relevant match (exact topic or concept)
  • 75-89%: Strong match (related concepts)
  • 60-74%: Moderate match (tangentially related)
  • Below 60%: Weak match (may not be relevant)

Scores are calculated using AI embeddings that understand meaning, not just keywords!

Example: Searching "machine learning" will also find memories about "neural networks" and "AI models" even if they don't contain the exact phrase.

üéØ Hybrid Search

Memory search uses both methods simultaneously:

  • Semantic Search: AI understands meaning and context (finds related concepts)
  • Keyword Search: Finds exact word matches in titles and content

This gives you the best of both worlds - precise keyword matches AND conceptually related results.

‚úçÔ∏è Writing Effective Prompts

üéØ The Prompt Formula

PROMPT = [WHO/WHAT] + [ACTION] + [CONTEXT] + [STYLE/TONE] + [CONSTRAINTS]

Formula Breakdown

1Ô∏è‚É£ WHO/WHAT

  • "A professional teacher..." ‚Üí for educational content
  • "A cybersecurity expert..." ‚Üí for technical analysis
  • "An enthusiastic storyteller..." ‚Üí for creative writing

2Ô∏è‚É£ ACTION

  • "Explain..." / "Create..." / "Analyze..." / "Summarize..."
  • "Generate..." / "Design..." / "Compare..." / "Evaluate..."

3Ô∏è‚É£ CONTEXT

  • "...for 8th-grade students studying photosynthesis..."
  • "...based on this YouTube video about WW2..."
  • "...for a corporate presentation on Q4 sales..."

4Ô∏è‚É£ STYLE/TONE

How should it sound or look?

  • "...in a friendly, conversational tone..."
  • "...with formal academic language..."
  • "...using simple vocabulary for beginners..."
  • (Images) "...photorealistic, 8K, cinematic lighting..."

5Ô∏è‚É£ CONSTRAINTS

  • "...in 3-5 bullet points"
  • "...under 200 words"
  • "...include 3 specific examples"
  • "...suitable for a 10-minute presentation"

‚ùå Bad vs ‚úÖ Good Examples

Example 1: Chat

‚ùå Bad: "Tell me about WW2"

Problem: Too vague, no context, no audience level, no focus

‚úÖ Good: "You are a high school history teacher. Explain the causes of World War 2 to 10th-grade students in a 5-minute lesson. Use simple language, include 3 key causes, and relate each to modern events they might understand. Keep it under 300 words."

Why it's better: Defines persona (teacher), action (explain), context (10th grade, 5 min), tone (simple), constraints (3 causes, 300 words)

Example 2: Image Generation

‚ùå Bad: "a cat"

Problem: No detail, no style, no context

‚úÖ Good: "A fluffy orange tabby cat sitting on a sunny windowsill, looking outside at falling snow. Warm indoor lighting, soft focus background, cozy atmosphere, photorealistic, 8K quality, professional pet photography"

Why it's better: Specific subject (orange tabby), action (sitting, looking), context (windowsill, snow), style (photorealistic, 8K), mood (cozy, warm)

Example 3: Research

‚ùå Bad: "Research AI"

Problem: Too broad, no focus, unclear intent

‚úÖ Good: "Conduct a comprehensive research analysis on how AI is being used in K-12 education as of 2024. Focus on: (1) Current adoption rates, (2) Most popular tools, (3) Effectiveness studies, (4) Teacher concerns. Provide cited sources and summarize key findings in a 2-page report format."

  • Memory sync requires cloud storage (Supabase)

  • However, your API keys and settings are stored locally

üìä What is DOK (Depth of Knowledge)?

DOK is an educational framework with 4 levels:

  • DOK 2: Skills & Concepts (apply knowledge)
  • DOK 3: Strategic Thinking (analyze, reason)
  • DOK 4: Extended Thinking (complex projects)

Video tools automatically generate assessments at different DOK levels. See Video Intelligence section.

üí° Optimization Tips

Staying Within Limits

  • Long Videos: Use Quick Summary instead of Full Analysis for 2+ hour videos
  • Large Transcripts: Video tools automatically chunk transcripts - works seamlessly
  • Many Images: Generate 1-2 at a time, wait for completion before next batch
  • Concurrent Tasks: Spread tasks across tabs to avoid rate limits
  • Memory Storage: Old memories are automatically archived (not deleted) if approaching limit
